About The Tensor Lab

The Tensor Lab for Computational Medicine is a collective of students, researchers, and physicians who volunteer their time, skills, and passion for medical research. We function as an enhancement to the medical student research experience, designed to train future physicians and AI leaders to conduct reproducible, high-impact clinical research that translates from bench to bedside.

The Critical Gap

The AI revolution is fundamentally reshaping clinical practice, yet a critical gap exists:

The Skills Gap

Most physicians lack the technical expertise to validate and deploy AI, while AI researchers often lack the necessary clinical context.

The Relevance Gap

Many AI projects lack real-world application; brilliant algorithms do not translate to better patient care without proper clinical context.

Tensor Lab exists to cultivate physician-scientists who speak both languages, producing rigorous research that translates AI from the lab to the bedside.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a medical background to apply as a Fellow?

No! We look for strong technical skills (Python, ML frameworks). Your Med Mentor will provide the necessary clinical context.

What is the time commitment?

The summer cohort is an intensive 10-week sprint requiring approx. 20-30 hours/week. During the school year, it varies by project.

Is this paid?

Currently, this is an unpaid educational fellowship focused on research output, authorship, and career development.

Who owns the intellectual property?

We believe in Open Science. Code is typically open-sourced, and data privacy is strictly managed by the PI's institution.
