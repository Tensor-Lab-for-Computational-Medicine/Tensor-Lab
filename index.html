About The Tensor Lab

The Tensor Lab for Computational Medicine is a volunteer collaborative of students, researchers, and physicians dedicated to advancing medical education. Our mission: train the next generation of physicians and AI leaders to conduct reproducible, high-impact clinical research that translates discoveries from bench to bedside.

The Tensor Lab Fellowship bridges code and clinic. During a 10-week summer program, Computer Science students partner with clinical mentors to solve real-world healthcare challenges. By embedding technical talent directly into clinical teams, we develop translational leaders capable of conducting independent, high-impact research.

The Critical Gap

The AI revolution is fundamentally reshaping clinical practice, yet a critical gap exists:

The Skills Gap

Most physicians lack the technical expertise to validate and deploy AI, while AI researchers often lack the necessary clinical context.

The Relevance Gap

Many AI projects lack real-world application; brilliant algorithms do not translate to better patient care without proper clinical context.

Medical Mentor

Clinical Mentorship & Publication Guidance

The Med Mentor bridges the gap between clinical needs and technical execution. You provide the question; we provide the engineering team.

Responsibilities

  • Scope Definition: Identify a specific clinical problem and submit a project proposal.
  • Team Selection: Interview and select the best technical fellows for your project.
  • Clinical Guidance: Ensure clinical rigor and frame the research question.
  • Publication Leadership: Serve as co-first author and lead manuscript preparation.

Qualifications

  • Academic Status: M1/M2 students with a dedicated research block.
  • Role Suitability: Future physician-scientists aiming to develop AI literacy alongside clinical judgment.
  • Outcome: Guaranteed co-first authorship on clinical papers.

2025 Pilot Cohort: Selected Works

Deep dives into the projects delivered by our first cohort.

Benchmarking / ICU Data UCSF

LLMs vs. Traditional ML for Clinical Prediction

PI: Dr. Jonas De Almeida
Mentor: Aaron Ge
Fellow: Jialong

Compared the performance of LLM-based semantic models (using LLM embeddings) against traditional ML models (XGBoost) across different clinical prediction tasks.

Result: Text representation critically affects performance; traditional ML excels at physiological data.
XGBoost, LLM Embeddings, Python
Pharmacogenomics UMSOM

Oncology Pharmacogenomics Knowledge Graph

PI: Dr. Brian Schulte
Mentors: Diem My, Chy Murali
Fellow: Dmitry

Developed the OPMG prototype to accelerate clinical evidence synthesis using an ETL-First strategy and Knowledge Graph RAG system.

Result: Validated ETL-first strategy and end-to-end KG-RAG pipeline functionality.
Knowledge Graph, RAG, Gemini, ETL
Oncology / SDOH UCSF

Modeling Prostate Cancer Using SDOH

PI: Dr. Scott Campbell
Mentors: Chy Murali, Diem My
Fellow: Malavika

Trained ML models using SEER registry data linked with Social Determinants of Health proxies to stratify prostate cancer patients.

Result: SDOH adds measurable predictive value, highlighting the importance of social context.
Logistic Regression, SDOH Data, Python
Head & Neck Cancer UCSF

RFS Prediction with/without Segmentation

PI: Dr. Grace Snow
Mentor: Laura Chen
Fellow: Koushik

Investigated whether a simplified multimodal model using only PET/CT and clinical data could achieve comparable RFS prediction to expert-annotated models.

Result: Simpler PET/CT model achieved comparable performance and is more generalizable.
Multimodal ML, PET/CT, Survival Analysis
Emergency Medicine Kaiser

LLM-Powered ER Triage Simulator

PI: Dr. Ian McLachlan
Mentor: Aaron Ge
Fellow: Ashwin

Developed a training simulator using LLMs to create interactive, data-grounded emergency triage encounters from MIMIC-IV data.

Result: Confirmed feasibility of using LLMs for realistic, scalable medical training tools.
LLMs, MIMIC-IV, Interactive Systems
Cardiology / Readmission UCSF

Predicting Hospital Readmissions

PI: Dr. Geoff Tison
Mentor: Griffith Hesketh
Fellow: Shravya

Developed an attention-based deep learning model (BioClinicalBERT) to predict 30-day readmissions from free-text discharge summaries.

Result: Achieved AUROC 0.730 with interpretable predictions via SHAP.
BioClinicalBERT, SHAP, NLP
Lung Cancer UCSF

Deep Learning for CT Lung Classification

PI: Dr. Julian Hong
Mentor: Matt Kim
Fellow: Juan Arturo

Compared ResNet50, VGG16, and InceptionV3 for CT-based lung lesion classification using the IQ-OTH/NCCD dataset.

Result: InceptionV3 showed the most consistent performance (0.92 F1-Score) for diagnostics.
InceptionV3, Computer Vision, CNNs

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a medical background to apply as a Fellow?

No! We look for strong technical skills (Python, ML frameworks). Your Med Mentor will provide the necessary clinical context.

What is the time commitment?

The summer cohort is an intensive 10-week sprint requiring approx. 20-30 hours/week. During the school year, it varies by project.

Is this paid?

Currently, this is an unpaid educational fellowship focused on research output, authorship, and career development.

Who owns the intellectual property?

We believe in Open Science. Code is typically open-sourced, and data privacy is strictly managed by the PI's institution.
