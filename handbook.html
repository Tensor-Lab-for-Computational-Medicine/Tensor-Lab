Operations Manual

The comprehensive guide to the Tensor Lab operating cadence, from recruitment to publication.

Medical Student Recruitment

January – February

January

Medical students can apply to be Chapter Directors and/or Med Mentors.

  • Chapter Directors (Due Jan 30): Qualified applicants will be invited for a brief interview with National Directors to gauge fit.
  • Med Mentors (Due Feb 28): Applicants must have a research proposal and PI. If they don’t, they can indicate if they’d like assistance connecting with one.

February

National Directors will review all Chapter Director applicants and finalize two "Trios" of Chapter Directors (Executive, Operations, Technical), one trio for each school.

Chapter Directors will be onboarded. Their first task is to assist the National Directors with Med Mentor recruitment and coordination (since they are classmates).

Project Development

March

Med Mentor Onboarding

Med Mentors will be formally onboarded into the chapter workflow.

  • With Proposal/PI: If the Med Mentor already has a research proposal and PI, they join a training meeting hosted by Chapter Directors on how to onboard that PI as a Faculty Mentor.
  • Without PI: The Chapter Directors will assist the Med Mentor in finding one. National Directors have access to a PI network and will communicate these opportunities to Chapter Directors.

Backup Plans

If a Med Mentor’s primary project has been accepted, their team must also propose an open-access, IRB-exempt backup project in case the primary data source hits roadblocks.

ML Fellow Recruitment

March – April

March Timeline

  • March 1: National Directors use major university listservs to recruit ML Fellows.
  • March 6: Approved projects are posted on the website for ML Fellow applicants to review.
  • Applications Open: Potential ML Fellows can indicate the specific Teams they want to work with from the project list.
  • Rolling Selection: Chapter Directors will select top candidates for each team’s Interview Pool on a rolling basis.
  • March 24 – April 10: ML Fellow applicants interviews take place.

April Decision

April 6: ML Fellow application closes. Teams submit their preferences for their ML Fellows, and Chapter Directors make the final decisions on assignments.

Institutional Access

April – June

This is the "Valley of Death" for projects. Teams must clear these hurdles before summer starts.

  • Teams will submit IRBs.
  • Secure institutional access (badges, VPNs).
  • Verify access to datasets is live and working.

Chapter Leadership

Each school (UMSOM & UCSF) is led by a "Trio" responsible for talent recruitment, local execution of the 10-week fellowship, and institutional compliance.
